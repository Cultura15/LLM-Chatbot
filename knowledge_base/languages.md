# Japanese Language & Culture

## Writing Systems

- **Hiragana** (46 characters): Used for native Japanese words, grammatical elements
- **Katakana** (46 characters): Used for foreign words, emphasis
- **Kanji**: Chinese characters (~2,000+ in regular use), each has meaning and multiple readings
- **Romaji**: Latin alphabet representation (used in this dictionary)

## Japanese Writing Tips

- Hiragana flows like cursive — each character is one syllable
- Katakana is angular — used for loanwords like コンピューター (computer)
- Kanji have on'yomi (Chinese reading) and kun'yomi (Japanese reading)
- Example: 山 = "san" (on) or "yama" (kun) — both mean "mountain"

## Grammar Basics

- **Word order**: Subject-Object-Verb (SOV) — "I sushi eat"
- **Particles**: が (ga) marks subject, を (wo) marks object, に (ni) marks direction
- ** Politeness**: Plain form (辞書形) vs Polite form (です/ます)
- **No plural**: "人" can mean "person" or "people"
- **No articles**: No "the" or "a" in Japanese

## Useful Phrases

- お願いします (onegaishimasu) — Please / May I have...
- ありがとうございます (arigatou gozaimasu) — Thank you (polite)
- すみません (sumimasen) — Excuse me / Sorry
- わかりません (wakarimasen) — I don't understand
- わかりました (wakarimashita) — I understand

## Japanese Culture Notes

- **Bowing**: Shows respect; deeper bow = more respect
- **Shoes off**: Remove shoes when entering homes, temples, some restaurants
- **Chopsticks**: Never stick chopsticks upright in rice (funeral ritual)
- **Gift giving**: Always wrap gifts; avoid sets of 4 (bad luck)
- **Slurping noodles**: Considered polite — shows enjoyment

## Days of the Week

- Monday: 月曜日 (getsuyoubi)
- Tuesday: 火曜日 (kayoubi)
- Wednesday: 水曜日 (suiyoubi)
- Thursday: 木曜日 (mokuyoubi)
- Friday: 金曜日 (kinyoubi)
- Saturday: 土曜日 (doyoubi)
- Sunday: 日曜日 (nichiyoubi)

## Japanese Holidays

- **正月 (Shogatsu)**: New Year — most important holiday
- **成人式 (Seijinshiki)**: Coming of Age Day (2nd Monday of January)
- **桜 (Sakura)**: Cherry blossom season — hanami (flower viewing)
- **祭り (Matsuri)**: Summer festivals with fireworks (花火, hanabi)
- **紅葉 (Kouyou)**: Autumn leaves viewing
